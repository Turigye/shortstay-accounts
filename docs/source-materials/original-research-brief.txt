Based on the official terms you shared and how the system is engineered, here is the direct breakdown of how your data is handled and how you can use it without giving the government constant monitoring access.

1. Do they keep your data on the web platform?
Yes, if you use the online portal.
The disclaimer notes that all data entered into the web version is securely stored on their servers to generate your automated reports (Income Statements, Balance Sheets, etc.). While it states that the data is protected against unauthorized access and should not be shared with third parties, it is stored in a database managed under the joint initiative of the Ministry of Finance, URA, and UNCTAD.

2. Is URA actively monitoring your live data?
No, the tool does not act as an active wiretap or a live fiscal device (like an EFRIS system that logs every single invoice in real-time).

Instead, the disclaimer explicitly points out that it features tax simulations and serves as a guidance and training tool to help you figure out how to formalize. However, because the web version requires you to log in with your actual contact details and business profile, the data sits on a government-supported platform.

3. How to use it with 100% Privacy (The Offline Loophole)
If you like the way the Easy Accounts questionnaire simplifies bookkeeping but absolutely do not want your financial data sitting on a government web server, do not sign up for the online portal.

Instead, use the Offline Spreadsheet version:

Zero Server Storage: The offline tool is a standalone file (usually Excel or a similar spreadsheet format) that runs entirely on your local computer.

Total Control: You feed your data locally into your own device. The formulas inside the file generate the exact same financial statements and tax simulations instantly on your screen.

No Uploads Required: Because it doesn’t sync to the internet, no one at URA or the Ministry of Finance can see your day-to-day cash flow, supplier debts, or profit margins. You only hand over data to URA manually when you choose to file your official tax returns.
