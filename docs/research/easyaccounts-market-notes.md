# Short-Stay Accounting Market Notes

## Product direction

The strongest market position is not another general accounting package. It is a private, fast desktop operating ledger for a small short-stay property: manual booking entry, payment allocation, daily unit status, expenses, activity-based staff compensation, and month-end reports in one workflow.

EasyAccounts Uganda keeps accounting approachable through a monthly questionnaire and produces income statements, balance sheets, and profit analysis. That validates simplicity as the right product principle, but a monthly, generic workflow does not give this client the daily booking, payment-balance, unit, and staff-allocation visibility they need.

Broader products such as Zoho Books cover invoicing, banking, expenses, inventory, projects, automation, and reporting. They are capable, but their breadth and cloud-first setup add overhead for a two-unit, owner-operated business. Short-Stay Accounts should stay property-first and local-first while exporting a complete workbook for accountants.

## Recommended differentiation

- One-screen daily command center for arrivals, departures, balances due, occupancy, cash position, staff allocation, tax provision, and expenses.
- Manual entry optimized for a two-unit operation, with no dependency on Airbnb or another booking platform.
- Percentage-based staff allocation driven by collected booking revenue, with effective-dated rates and an audit trail.
- Encrypted local business file, encrypted backups, password-gated restore, and accountant-ready Excel export.
- Full reports without exposing bookkeeping jargon during ordinary daily entry.

## Uganda rental tax

For an individual landlord, URA states that annual rental tax is 12% of annual gross rental income above the UGX 2,820,000 threshold, with no other deductions. The app treats UGX 600,000 per active unit per month as the configurable gross rental-income basis. For two units, this produces annual gross rental income of UGX 14,400,000, estimated annual rental tax of UGX 1,389,600, and a monthly provision of UGX 115,800.

This is an estimate, not a filed return or assessment. Companies use a different rental-tax calculation, so this implementation assumes the client is taxed as an individual landlord.

URA describes EFRIS as the system for fiscal invoices and receipts. VAT-registered taxpayers are required to use it, while non-VAT taxpayers are encouraged to use it voluntarily. EFRIS integration is therefore a separate future compliance scope and depends on the business's taxpayer status.
